Neuroplasticity

Understanding neuroplasticity is good for learners as they understand or can realise that although they don't know or understand a concept or idea doesn't mean they are dumb and instead they realise they just need to work at it and practice until they understand the skill.
An example of engaging in neuroplasticity in my life would be to do with videogames. When you start a game you don't even know the controls although most games share similar trigger and button values.
They almost never have the same techniques or even similar timing when it comes to doing things . So learning which ones to press comes with time. Often when starting a game my first few hours I will make mistakes because I haven't learnt what buttons to press to get the right attack or haven't learnt the timing on a dodge.
By the end of the game however I'm generally very good in comparison to the beginning of the game because of the constant practice and repetition. I also notice that when I leave a game for a while I lose the level of ability I previously had. But a quick recap jogs the memory and it takes substantially less time to rise up again.
A great way to increase your neuroplasticity is to practise repeatedly and frequently.

Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is where you can realise your not very good at something and instead of having a fixed mindset where you would think you can not get better and you won't be good at it you see your not the best but accept the challenge of getting better It's good to have a growth mindset as your more likely to do the work even when you don't understand it.
A growth mindset is all about determination. I realised whilst researching that I used to have an extremely fixed mindset and that it only really changed when I got to NCEA and I had to put a lot of effort in when I realised that doing something whilst not fully understanding is better than giving up or not giving it your all because you think youll never understand.
Im definitely integrating that growth mindset when working the past few weeks as ive been getting alot of information and not that much time to do it. so far i've been pretty good at using this mindset.
